Introduction

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is a renowned international cricketer celebrated for his exceptional batting prowess. Displaying promise from a young age, Kohli captained the Indian Under-19 team to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup. Making his debut for the Indian national team in 2008, he quickly rose to prominence with his aggressive style of play and an unwavering work ethic. Kohli assumed the captaincy of the Indian cricket team in Tests in 2014 and later took charge of the limited-overs formats. Under his leadership, the team achieved notable successes, including historic Test series victories in Australia. Kohli holds numerous records, including being the fastest to reach milestones in One Day Internationals (ODIs). Recognized with prestigious awards like the ICC Cricketer of the Year and the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, Kohli is not only a cricket icon but also actively engages in philanthropy. He is married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, and together they have a daughter named Vamika. Kohli's enduring impact on the cricketing world and his commitment to social causes have solidified his status as one of India's most influential sports personalities.
